SOUND ARCHITECT

Sound is usually treated as something that happens in the background. In reality, it influences how people interpret space, brands, and presence.

What I focus on is not the production of sound, but the way sound behaves when no one is governing it.

Observation

In most environments, sound is not designed as a system. It emerges from disconnected decisions, habits, and aesthetic choices.

Over time, this creates a layer of inconsistency that people feel but rarely articulate.

The real issue is not sound quality, but lack of coherence.

Perspective

My background moves between architecture, live environments, and long-term observation of how people respond to space and sound.

This led me to stop thinking in terms of audio content, and start thinking in terms of systems of perception.

Sound Governance

Sound Governance is not a style or a design approach. It is a way of bringing consistency to something that is usually fragmented.

It defines how sound behaves across contexts, time, and environments.

When sound is not structured, it still influences perception — just without intention.
